WHY DID WE START THIS?

The main reason to why we started this was, we came from a middle class family where we were surrounded by families who had issues financially, and found that, sending a boy to a college or a school was better than sending a girl to one.Hence the start of this scholarship program.Therefore we aim to:

  1. Empower: Education is a powerful tool for empowerment. A girls' scholarship can empower young women by providing them with the skills, knowledge, and confidence needed to make informed decisions about their lives, pursue their aspirations, and become leaders in their communities.
  2. Global Development Goals: Scholarships for girls align with international development goals, such as the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Goal 4 focuses on quality education, and goal 5 emphasizes gender equality and empowerment of women and girls.
  3. Diversity and Inclusion: A diverse and inclusive society benefits from the talents, perspectives, and contributions of all its members. A girls' scholarship program contributes to creating a more inclusive educational environment and workforce.
  4. Long-Term Societal Benefits: The benefits of educating girls extend to future generations. Educated mothers are more likely to prioritize education for their own children, creating a positive cycle of educational attainment and progress.

WHO SUPPORT US IN THIS JOURNEY?

Even though this an NGO, we do face some difficulties when it comes to sponsorship but we wouldn't be here if not for these few sponsors who helped us till now and what they do to help us:

  1. Malala Fund: Named after Nobel laureate Malala Yousafzai, this organization advocates for girls' education and provides funding for girls' education projects and scholarships in various countries.
  2. Girl Rising: Girl Rising is a global campaign that uses the power of storytelling to advocate for girls' education. They partner with organizations and individuals to fund scholarships and educational programs for girls.
  3. Room to Read: This nonprofit organization focuses on improving literacy and gender equality in education. They offer girls' education programs, including scholarships, to support girls in their educational journey.
  4. Global Fund for Women: While not exclusively focused on education, this organization supports women's and girls' rights around the world. They fund initiatives that aim to empower girls through education, leadership, and advocacy.
  5. She's the First: This organization supports girls' education in low-income countries and helps girls become the first in their families to graduate from high school and college. They provide scholarships and mentoring programs.
  6. The Circle: This organization of women supports various projects related to women's rights and empowerment, including education. They offer grants and support initiatives that benefit girls' education.
  7. Rotary International: Many local Rotary Clubs and the Rotary Foundation are committed to supporting education. They might offer scholarships or partner with other organizations on educational initiatives, including those focused on girls.
  8. Zonta International: Zonta is a global organization of professionals dedicated to empowering women. They support various projects aimed at advancing women's rights and education, including scholarships for girls.
  9. Local Businesses and Corporations: Many local businesses and corporations have corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives that focus on education and gender equality. You can approach them for potential partnerships or sponsorships
